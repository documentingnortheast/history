The West Boldon Wild Bunch

The West Boldon Wild Bunch (WBWB) were a gang centred in the Boldon Colliery area, active during the years 1987–1993. The bunch are most well known for jumping over the River Don, in the Colliery, as a means to assert their dominance over the area. They also were involved in various petty crimes and although centered in the Colliery their crimes went as far as East Boldon and Cleadon. At their peak, their membership reached 8.

Origins

In the early 1980s, soon to be leader of the gang "Cooky", would reportedly jump over the River Don alone, repeatedly. On a slightly overcast day Payney approached Cooky jumping over the Don, among a brief conversation (which isn't publicly known), Payney became enticed in the practice of jumping over the Don and would from that day on continue to jump over the Don with Cooky, all day. It is widely recognised that when Payney joined Cooky in this action is the beginning of the gang.

It is among growing consensus that Cooky believed that continually jumping over the Don would assert their dominance over the area, deterring enemy gangs, such as the FMF (Fast Fry Firm).

Members

Cooky

Cooky, was the founder of the Bunch, he spent much of his early years submerged in the polluted waters of the Don, leaving only his nose above the surface at the deepest point of the Don. He would spend so much time in the Don that his body began to discolour with close family recalling his body was "green and blue", likely due to the prolonged exposure to the unknown substances present in the Don.

Payney

Payney, the co-founder of the group, was a polymath with a reported IQ of over 194. Acting as the brains of the Bunch he planned heists like the West Boldon School Job and the Cleadon Copper Graft Job. Nothing is known about his family, only that he had a brown dog. In school he scored full marks on all of his O-Level exams, claiming to a friend "he didn't even try."

Danny

Danny, was the third of the group to join, he did not go to school finding work as a local chimney sweep in the area. After several months as a sweep he joined the group. He had an abnormally loud scream, which would become vital in the Cleadon Copper Graft Job. Among an interview in jail, he recalled "no one could come close to my primal scream". Danny could commonly be found climbing trees overhanging the Don, then fall into the shallow water below, screaming "Ooga Booga" as he fell.

Gilesy

Gilesy, was a strong swimmer, he quit his job as a film rental clerk to pursue his hobby of swimming. Due to fear of tides he would never swim in the sea, leading him to make do with the shallow waters of The Don. Although passing the Bunch jumping over him as he swam through the Don, he would pay no attention or even acknowledge the gang, likely due to the pain of the rocks of the bed of the Don. He would be forced into service in the gang after he stole meatloaf from Stoney's bakery, but became a trusted and loyal member of the gang a few years later.

Stoney

Stoney, was a notable cricketer and baker in East Boldon. He worked in a bakery specializing in goods like sourdough bread and meatloaf, current establishment Blacks Corner sells a product named "Stoney's Sourdough" in honor of his recipe. After work he could be found playing cricket at the local club, rumours suggest that he would put an entire cricket ball into his mouth, spit it out and hit it with his bat. He joined the gang after stumbling among the bunch by The Don after trying to retrieve a lost cricket ball.

Zanny

Zanny, was an immigrant from Sri Lanka, he brought 45kg of coconuts with him starting his own business selling coconut water. According to his siblings he was "addicted" to said coconut water. Employees of his coconut water stall recalled he would "down all the stock" and if rumours are to be believed would bathe in the coconut water - although this is likely to be propaganda spread by the rival gang, The Fast Fry Firm.

Massy

Massy, was the son of an army man and was deeply interested in the armed forces and wanted to enlist into the Falklands War, which he couldn't do due to not meeting eyesight requirements. Among being rejected he would practice his low crawl throughout the full distance of the Don, which would be against the flow of the stream. It was among these low crawl endeavours he would find the gang jumping over the Don, enlisting shortly after.

Gibbaz

Gibbaz, was the 7th and last son of famed bricklayer Barry. Gibbaz in his early years played vital roles in the construction of various homes and paths throughout all the Colliery up to Cleadon - pioneering restoration works to Cleadon Windmill. Gibbaz was also a great rock collector, with many of his collections being sold in the Auction House in Boldon. Gibbaz would join the gang in the late 90s, among being fired from his construction job after an incident after he clogged a clients toilet after having a curry from the Eastern Touch.

Early activities

The WBWB's early activities from 1987 to 1989, is largely unknown due to their unprolific nature and as their crimes couldn't be attributed to one singular gang. The following are the only credible activities of the gang, based on eyewitness testimonies of sightings of the gang members:

  • A house robbery, which they stole a fax machine and toilet paper.
  • A failed robbery of The Wheatsheaf - which all members escaped.
  • The vandalism of Boldon School, where the letters "WBWB" were painted in big black letters on the school's walls.
  • The vandalism of Grange Park, where the bunch cut down 2 trees.
  • The robbery of The Wheatsheaf, the second attempt which resulted in them stealing lots of stock and the safe from the office.

The Wheatsheaf robbery marked a turning point in the gang's ambitions, they got their first taste of money, and began setting their eyes on more prolific targets which ironically would lead to their downfall.

The gang is likely to have committed much more crimes like these but can't be attributed to them as there is no significant evidence linking them to the crimes.

Prime Years 1989 - 1992

After thier first taste of cash from the 2nd robbery of The Wheatsheaf the Bunch now set their eyes among greater targets. The prime years now included members Gibbaz and Zanny. The gang robbed over 15 houses in these years, robbed multiple stores and cars. However, none come close to the greatness of the Cleadon Copper Graft Job and The West Boldon School Robbery.

The Cleadon Copper Graft Job

The job came into planning in mid 1989, with Payney planning the job, to rob an electrical firm. The scoping out was carried out by Cooky, which he submerged himself in the pond close to the firm, watching movement in and out of the firm. Original plans, sculputured by Cooksy was for the gang to get jobs at the electrical firm and simply being paid, effectively robbing the bank without commiting a crime. The original plan was sidelined by Payney due to the hard nature of all members getting a job at the firm. The job was scheduled for November 4th 1989 but was postponed due Payney loosing his dog, the job would officialy commence on the night of November 19th 1989 and the early hours of November 20th.

Danny was assigned distraction duty, and at approximately 11pm let out an uninteligable primal scream, the noise attracted most of the workers sleeping overnight in the firm. The rest of the gang was split up into 2 groups. The first group consisted of Cooky, Gilesy, Payney and Massy and the second group Stoney, Danny, Zanny and Gibbaz.

In the pitch black the first group descended onto the firm, climbed the fence and made there way into the side entrance. The door was only locked by zip tie, easily broke by the group. Massy was the last to enter as he was low crawling the entire distance of the car park. Once in, the first group looted the east side of the firm, aiming for copper piping and wiring but took most they could fit in their 100L bags.

The second group would come into the firm much later due to complications, the first one arising with Stoney - who was disguised as a cricket umpire started communicating in entirely cricket jargon. The second would come from Zanny, who was hyper due to the amount of coconut water he drank in the hours prior. The gang snuck through a hole in the fence on the west side of the firm, approaching the wall. Gibbaz was tasked with breaking through the wall, using his construction skills this was done with ease and silently. The second group was in and took copper piping and computer parts.

Both groups exited the firm at approximately 1am, undetected. The gang was able to regroup with Danny an walk back to their base of operations in the Colliery. The gang was able to sell their goods over the next few months to a japanese company. The stolen goods, which included 18kg of copper wiring, 6 CPUs and other miscellaenous items, would amount to an estimated total of £12,000 (£36,000 adjusted for inflation).

West Boldon School Robbery

Inbetween petty robberies conducted by the gang, Payney recieved a tip from West Boldon School has just received a brand-new shipment of computers and tech equipment. Payney planned for the gang to break into the school summer holidays and rob the school of its new shipment and existing tech like printers and keyboards.

Payney created fake paperwork claiming they are from “Northern Educational Electrical Solutions STD" and would need to conduct emergency work on the tech equipment due to a fire risk, caused by the new shipment. On the 3rd of August 1991, the gang would break into the school using the following method; Gibbaz led the pack and managed to pick the lock on the front school gates whilst Zanny attempted to create a diversion by setting up a coconut water stall outsdie the gates, which wasn't needed as no one was there. The job was swifter than the Cleadon Copper Graft Job, according to an interview with Gilesy in jail "we were proppa rapid mush". The gang escaped the school without setting off any alarms, they stole 93% of exposed electrical equipment, totalling to an estimated £82,000 (£380,000 adjusted for inflation). The gang managed to sell off most of their items, to close friends and local tech dumps. It's speculated the gang may have kept some of the equipment for themselves, likely keeping the photocopier so they could photocopy pictures of their bums and place it among rival gang The Fast Fry Firm's territory.

However, the West Boldon School Robbery, although a peak for the gang, would also mark it's downfall. The owner of the School, Sggarc - a rich businessman, would be extremely agitated from this robbery, he hired a private detective agency, The Ew Enterprise, owned by Imed Ew. The gang would be pressured and closely followed in the following years, playing a big role in their eventual downfall.

Downfall 1993

Although the gang had just come of the back of a huge score, internal divisions and external pressures from The Ew Enterprise would ultimately lead to the gangs downfall.

Imediately after the West Boldon School Robbery, Sggarc hired The Ews to look into the WBWB. Quickly, the Ews would find leads, the most notable breakthrough coming in early Janurary 1993 where they managed to locate Zanny's back alley coconut stall. After a sting operation, Zanny was taken into police custody at HMP Durham. Zanny was questioned hard about the whereabouts of the gang, initially he didn't crack until months of Chinese water drop torture. He revealled the location of the gangs base - which is still private infomation held by the police. Among hearing of Zanny's capture the gang were troubled, they had never experienced anything like this before and when the Ews came poking about near there base, they knew Zanny had cracked.

Massy, Stoney and Danny proposed a plan to break Zanny out of prison, but the plan was rejected by Cooky. This lead to deep internal divisions within the gang, with the three believing that Cooky had lost faith in Zanny and the gang as a whole. Cooky over the next few weeks would become more troubled, drinking heavily alone which impaired his decision making and hindered his temper, consistently lashing out at the gang. After an intense arguement on February 24th Massy, Stoney, Gibbaz, Danny and Gilesy would leave the base of operations in an anger, leaving only Cooky and Payney in the base.

The Ews would find a trail of Zanny's coconut water, leading them directly to the base of the WBWB only now with Cooky and Payney inside, with the rest of the gang absent. On February 26th the Ews would break into the base and after a brief standoff with the two, both Cooky and Payney would be arrested. The WBWB base was searched thouroughly and although many belongings to the absent gang members, none could be used to trace them of their whereabouts. The absent gang members likely heard about the arrests in the newspaper and chose not to come back, moving elsewhere, likely Butlins Skegness or Bognor.
